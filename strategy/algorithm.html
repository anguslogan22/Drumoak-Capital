Introducing the “Buffett Bounce” Strategy

Live soon on Drumoak Capital

Idea: When Berkshire Hathaway discloses a new equity position, the market often responds with follow-on buying. Investors anchor to Buffett’s reputation and implied cost basis, creating short-term momentum that can persist for several sessions.

How it works

  • Signal: Confirmed Berkshire 13F/13G/13D filings or credible news of a new stake.
  • Entry: Systematic triggers after confirmation (no anticipatory trades).
  • Holding Period: ~5 trading days, targeting the initial post-disclosure momentum.
  • Risk Controls: Tight stop-loss, fixed position sizing, and time-based exits.

Recent example

UnitedHealth (UNH): After reports of a Berkshire position, shares bounced from the mid-$200s to above $300 as capital flowed in—precisely the kind of move this strategy seeks to capture.
