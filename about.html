Research Methodology

How We Build a Report

01

Frame the Central Debate

Before touching financials, we identify the single most important question the market is debating. This becomes the organising principle of the entire report — every section connects back to it.

02

Understand the Business

We study the competitive dynamics, unit economics, distribution model, and management quality. The goal is to develop a clear view on what makes the business durable — or fragile — before looking at reported numbers.

03

Anchor on the Right Metrics

Not all data is signal. We identify which KPIs best reflect the underlying health of the business — distinguishing between real demand signals and accounting or timing noise.

04

Build the Valuation Range

Rather than a single price target, we model a Base and Bull case — framing the range of outcomes across a 3–4 year earnings power horizon. The target price sits within that range with a clear rationale.

05

Define the Monitoring Framework

Every report ends with specific, measurable signals that would either strengthen or challenge the thesis. This is what separates a live investment process from a one-time write-up.

"The mandate is to develop a repeatable research process — one that is honest about uncertainty, disciplined about evidence, and improves with each report."
